# Alarm Power Button Snoozes Instead of Stopping

## Mentions: 4 commenters
## Severity: MEDIUM-HIGH

## Affected Products
- iOS

## Duration
- Years (ongoing)

## Description
When an alarm goes off, pressing the power/side button snoozes the alarm instead of stopping it. This is counterintuitive because:
- The same button stops phone calls
- The same button stops timers
- Users expect it to dismiss the alarm
- Results in oversleeping when users think they stopped the alarm

## User Reports
- "I've been late to work because I thought I turned off my alarm"
- "Why does the same button do different things for alarms vs calls?"
- "The UI doesn't make it clear that you're snoozing, not stopping"
- "I have to unlock my phone and find the tiny Stop button"

## Workarounds
- Swipe on the notification to stop (requires unlocking)
- Tap "Stop" button on screen (requires looking at phone)
- Disable snooze entirely in alarm settings
- Use a third-party alarm app

## Design Issue
This is arguably a UX design flaw rather than a bug, but the inconsistency with other iOS behaviors makes it feel broken.
